## **1. Identifying and Writing Problem Statements**

### **Overview:**

The goal of ‚ÄúFitLife‚Äù is to help users maintain a consistent fitness routine by tracking workouts, setting achievable goals, and visualizing progress through simple and motivating UI elements.

### **User Pain Points:**

* Users have **inconsistent fitness routines** due to lack of motivation and busy schedules.
* Most fitness apps are **too complex** with unnecessary features.
* Users can‚Äôt easily **track visual progress** (weight loss, muscle gain, or consistency).
* Notifications or reminders are either **too frequent or not personalized**.
* Beginners often **feel overwhelmed** by complicated workout data and charts.

### **Problem Statements:**

1. **Primary Problem:**
   *‚ÄúBusy working professionals need a way to track their daily workouts and progress because their tight schedules make it hard to stay consistent, which results in poor fitness habits and decreased motivation.‚Äù*

2. **Secondary Problem:**
   *‚ÄúBeginner fitness users need a simple way to visualize their fitness journey because complicated charts and data discourage them, which results in abandoning fitness goals.‚Äù*

---

## **2. Identifying Appropriate Research Methods**

### **Chosen Methods:**

#### **a. Surveys**

* **Purpose:** To collect quantitative data about user fitness goals, preferred workout styles, and frustrations with current apps.
* **Execution:** Online Google Form shared in college, gyms, and social media.
* **Example Questions:**

  * How many days per week do you exercise?
  * What feature do you use most in a fitness app?
  * What motivates you to continue working out?

#### **b. One-on-One Interviews**

* **Purpose:** To explore user emotions, motivations, and real experiences in depth.
* **Execution:** Conduct 8‚Äì10 semi-structured interviews (20‚Äì30 minutes each).
* **Example Questions:**

  * Tell me about your experience with fitness apps.
  * What frustrates you when tracking workouts?
  * How do you stay motivated to exercise?

#### **c. Usability Testing (Prototype Phase)**

* **Purpose:** To observe how users interact with the prototype and identify usability issues.
* **Execution:** Provide low-fidelity wireframes for 5 test participants.
* **Metrics:** Task completion rate, time to complete a workout log, and satisfaction level.

### **Justification:**

Surveys help capture broad user data, interviews provide emotional context, and usability testing validates real-world functionality.
This mix of methods ensures **user-centered design** that‚Äôs data-driven and empathetic.

## **1. Identifying and Writing Problem Statements**

### **Overview:**

‚ÄúStyleEase‚Äù is an online clothing platform that helps users find perfectly fitting and visually accurate products, reducing returns and improving satisfaction.

### **User Pain Points:**

* Users find **size charts confusing** across brands.
* **Product images** often don‚Äôt match the real item.
* **Return/refund processes** are slow and frustrating.
* Users struggle with **discovering relevant items** among too many options.
* Repeat buyers **waste time reordering** previously purchased products.

### **Problem Statements:**

1. **Primary Problem:**
   *‚ÄúOnline shoppers need a way to find clothes that fit their size accurately because size charts vary across brands, which results in frequent returns and disappointment.‚Äù*

2. **Secondary Problem:**
   *‚ÄúReturning customers need a quick way to reorder favorite items because re-browsing wastes time, which reduces loyalty and satisfaction.‚Äù*

---

## **2. Identifying Appropriate Research Methods**

### **Chosen Methods:**

#### **a. User Interviews**

* **Purpose:** Understand user frustrations with sizing, returns, and confidence in online purchases.
* **Execution:** Conduct 10 interviews with regular online shoppers.
* **Example Questions:**

  * How do you decide what size to order online?
  * What frustrates you about product listings or returns?
  * What would make you more confident buying clothes online?

#### **b. Card Sorting**

* **Purpose:** Improve navigation by organizing product categories the way users expect.
* **Execution:** Participants group clothing types (e.g., ‚ÄúTops,‚Äù ‚ÄúFormal,‚Äù ‚ÄúAccessories‚Äù).
* **Outcome:** Better menu structure and product filter system.

#### **c. A/B Testing**

* **Purpose:** Evaluate design decisions (e.g., new product page layout, ‚ÄúTry On‚Äù feature).
* **Execution:** Split traffic between two design versions and compare conversion rates.
* **Metrics:** Click-through rate, cart addition rate, checkout completion.

### **Justification:**

Interviews reveal emotional buying barriers, card sorting improves discoverability, and A/B testing validates real performance improvements ‚Äî ensuring both usability and profitability.
